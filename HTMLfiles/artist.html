Historical Artists

Leonardo Da Vinci

Leonardo was born on April 15, 1452 in Toscany, near Vinci, where he was raised by his grand-parents. Between 1482 and 1499, he was a painter, sculptor and architect at the court of the Sforzas in Milano. At this period, he painted the famous "Last Supper" which marked the beginning of the High Renaissance. In 1500, he painted the Mona-Lisa and was busy studying the human body at a hospital's mortuary. He died in France, on May 2, 1519.

Vincent van Gogh

Vincent van Gogh was born on March 30, 1853 in Groot-Zundert near Breda(Netherlands). From 1869, he worked on art market of Hague, London and Paris. In 1879, he decided to start painting under the goodwill of his cousin. In the city of Mauve, during 1883 to 1885, he painted and drew with ashen and dark colors. and introduced him as an impressionist painter. On July 27th, 1890, van Gogh shot himself with a gun and died 2 days later.

Raphael

Raphael was born on April 6, 1483. Between 1500 and 1504, he lived in Florence and was influenced by Michelangelo and da Vinci. Raphael made several portraits of known celebrities. Raphael is the one that best embodies this art style. His work has influenced several generations of artists worldwide. He died in 1520.
